THE IMPORTANCE OF RENEWABLE ENERGY

Renewable is enenrgy derived from natural sources that are replenished at a higher rate than they are consumed. Sunlight and wind for example, are such sources that are constantly being replenished . renewable energy sources are plentiful and all raound us, which we can use to replace Non-renewable energy.

So what is non-renewable energy and how much harm can it cause?

Non-renewable energy comes from sources that will run out in the future of our lifetimes. Most of non-renewable energy sources are fossil fuels: coal, petroleum, and natural gas. These form of energies when burnt release carbon dioxide into the atmosphere this causes the hole in the ozone layer becomes bigger, also research has shown that a major contributor to the green-house effect and global climate changes is our overuse of fossil fuels.

Global Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, which could cause bigger problems in the future such as:

  • Rise in sea level due to ice melting in Arctic
  • Extreme heat is some areas
  • Causing many species to become extinct as they can not adapt with weather changes.
    • and of course many other problems.
